GDP and Life Expectancy





The scatter plot visualize the relationship of GDP per Capita,
Life Expectancy and Population. From the plot, it is obvious that
when GDP per Capita of a country increases, Life Expectancy of that
country also increase. When grouping wih continent, Africa is having
the lowest Life Expectancy and GDP per Capita. This is most likely
that the there are various diseases had been ravaging Africa for a
long time which cause the high death rates and short life expectancy.
From that, they are always lack of people to develop their countries
and economic.

Among all the continents, Asia countries are having the most obvious
increasing trend in life expectancy and GDP per Capita. In 1995, the
life expectancy and GDP per Capita of almost half of Asia continent


only slightly better than Africa. When time increase, the GDP per
capita of most Asian countries is already comparable to that of
Europe and the United States. Growth was driven by rapid
industrialisation, often led by exports and linked with changes
in the composition of output and employment. It was supported by
coordinated economic policies, unorthodox wherever and whenever
necessary, across sectors and over time.

From 1995, the points started to move from bottom left corner to
upper right corner which shows the improve of both GDP per
Capita and Life Expectancy of countries. However, there are no
obvious relationship of population with life expectancy and GDP per
Capita. There are different population across different range of data.


In overall, the trend of life expectancy are increasing over year.
However, the increasing trend varies among different continent.
Africa is having the lowest life expectancy compared with other
continent. It is the only continent that the life expectancy is
always lower than 65 years. However, their life expectancy is
growing rapidly. Its live expectancy increase from 55 in 2000 to
63 in 2015 which is also the peak. The significant improve in life
expectancy in Africa may mainly due to the improvement in health
service. There are large variety of diseases spreading in Africa
in past. The improvement in health service helps in protect them
and cure the diseases. However, the life expectancy of Africa is
the lowest compared with other continent.

The improvement in life expectancy in other areas, as observed in
the chart, is relatively gradual and modest, with most continents
experiencing an increase of only 2 to 5 years over time. This
suggests that life expectancy has been improving steadily but at
a relatively slow pace in these regions. This might due to these
area had reached a stable developing stage in which the economic,
health care and demographic changes are already quite comprehensive.
